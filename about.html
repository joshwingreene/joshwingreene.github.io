Welcome!

The name's Joshwin and I'm a Design-minded Mobile/Web App Developer. I got my start in tech when I started to co-develop and spearhead the design of ScholarDev App's mobile bus schedule, which was called Fresno Transit Free. That application was launched in 2014, garnered over 15,000 downloads on Google Play, and had a 4/5 star rating as of 2016. While designing and developing this app, I cultivated a strong passion for the creation of great user experiences and design in general.

Here’s a picture of my team and I when we tied for People’s Choice in a local hackathon called 59DaysofCode in 2013:

In 2016, I graduated from University of California, Irvine with a B.S. in Software Engineering. However, after further reflection into how I felt about programming at the time and the experiences that I had with some technical interviews, I decided to explore a career in User Experience Design. For reference, at the time, this is what I would say when it came to programming:

"Even though I enjoy the fruits of my labor, I don't know if I enjoy the labor itself."

I would end up embarking on an independent user experience case study in order to help me get my foot in the door as a UX Design intern. You can read more about that project here:

In 2018, I was given the opportunity to design and develop the front-end for a contract labor startup. You can learn more about that project here:

In July, I left this project on amicable terms and put my time into competing in another iteration of 59DaysofCode with a product that would compete against Pocket and Instapaper. I had identified a need in the Save-for-Later space while conducting the above independent case study. I served as the project lead, product designer, and co-developer of the product’s front-end. We ended up coming in second place.

After the competition, I got the opportunity to come back to that contract labor project with my role being changed to where I would lead the design of both the mobile and web applications and support the development of both applications.

After that project ended in December of 2018, I decided to go all-in with user experience design by enrolling in Flatiron School's UX/UI Design Immersive program. Here's a picture of me and a few members of my cohort during our project showcase (we were seated):

It was a great experience, but after graduating and going back to work on a re-branded and redesigned version of the project that I entered into 59DaysofCode after exploring a couple of job opportunities, I wondered why I moved away from software engineering in the first place. Looking back it it now, it was my unhealthy expectations of what not doing well in a couple of top-tier interviews meant and me jumping on the bandwagon of getting a job where I enjoyed the majority of the work. At this point, this is how my view of programming has changed:

I enjoy the fruits of my labor and I don't mind doing it.

Here's something relevant that I said to someone in July about finding their purpose:

"...I would suggest listing your hobbies and choosing the one that you don’t find tedious and you don’t mind doing. Additionally, ask yourself if there is some greater purpose that you want to use that hobby to achieve. For me, that’s programming and I want to make a positive impact on society using software. The great thing is that another hobby of mine is user experience design. I love how I can combine it with programming and the result allows me to be able to do so much as I can develop solutions to solve problems that I stumble upon as I live my life."

When compared to UX Design, I find software engineering to be less tedious and more rewarding. As my skills improve and I gain more experience, this will mean that I will be able to bring more complex applications to life in the future.

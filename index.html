Greetings! My name is Joshwin Greene, and I’m a recent software engineering graduate from UC Irvine (Summer 2016). Even though I got a degree in software engineering, my intention is to become an expert UX Designer. However, I do plan on keeping up my development skills by being a indie app developer and entrepreneur on the side. 

I got my start in UX design when I started to spearhead the design of Fresno Transit Free, which is a mobile bus schedule for my hometown. That application has now garnered over 15,000 downloads on Google Play with a current rating of 4/5 stars. While designing and developing that application, I started to cultivate a passion for the creation of great user experiences and design in general.  

As a UX Designer, my main priority is to offer solutions that meet the needs of users, but which also reflect the needs of the business. Even though this isn’t always easy, I will do what’s necessary to ensure that both parties are satisfied and, hopefully, thrilled. It should be known that I’m organized, responsible, and proactive. These characteristics have often caused me to obtain a leadership role in the teams that I become a part of. For instance, for Fresno Transit Free, I was originally the team leader for the originating class project at my community college. For my senior design project at UC Irvine, I led development efforts based on my experience as a mobile app developer for ScholarDev LLC (the company that owns Fresno Transit Free). More recently, I have been leading the Logistics/Food commitee for Women Techmakers Fresno’s upcoming women’s tech conference. 

As a developer, I care about code readability, maintainability, and performance. My aim is to build apps that make a positive impact on people’s lives.

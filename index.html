Work Experience

Co-Founder / General Project Manager / Mobile App Developer - ScholarDev LLC (2012 - Present)

I co-founded ScholarDev LLC in the fall of 2012 with +Vanida Bounyarith, +Peggy Bounyarith, and +Homer Greene. ScholarDev LLC evolved from a student development team that was formed after my Advanced Programming Applications class (focus - Android development) that was taught at Fresno City College in the summer of 2011. The team, which was called ScholarDev (get it?), was formed out of our desire to continue working on the project that was started in the class and solve a problem in our local community. Here was our mission statement:

Our mission is to express our active citizenship of making a difference in our communities by providing technology that makes lives and transportation easier for everyone.

ScholarDev

The initial premise of our project was to create a prototype Android application that would serve the students of Fresno City College that utilize the Fresno Area Express (FAX) bus system. We decided to make it open to all of the bus riders in Fresno, and the culminating app became Fresno Transit Free, which was released for both Android and iOS in April of 2014.

ScholarDev LLC, also known as ScholarDev Apps, specializes in the development of mobile applications. Here is a picture of us:

Notable Projects

Fresno Transit Free ( iOS / Android )

A mobile bus schedule that improves the experience of Fresno Area Express bus riders.

Released

Fresno Transit Free’s goal is to improve the experience of Fresno Area Express bus riders (FAX), reduce paper trails, and lower costs for Fresno bus riders and FAX.

Fresno Transit Free allows bus riders to view depictions of each route that FAX serves; offer transit information, such as fare and transfer information; view arrival times for all major stops; save their most use routes for quick access; and quickly access Google Maps.

Mixagram

I joined this project last October and its goal was to take things up a notch with Vine-like experiences by adding music into the mix.

Mixagram would allow users to record video clips and combine them with music at the same time, view a feed of your “mixed” video clips, share video clips with popular social networks, and choose music from the user’s personal library or buy songs from iTunes or an alternate music repository.

There’s probably more features at this point, but I had to sadly leave the project in February to focus on my studies. However, I’m looking forward to getting my hands on the final result.

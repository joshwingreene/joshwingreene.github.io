Overview

Introduction

My teammates and I worked through a user-centered design process over the course of 6 weeks. In the end, we delivered a mobile, career resource guide. 

Here’s what was asked of us in the brief that we given:

Help public libraries strengthen their community relationships and smooth the disruption of technology on their communities by:

→ Improving patrons’ skills
→ Helping patrons find employment
→ Preparing young people for a new labor market

Team Collaboration Style

For each deliverable, we would generally assign a captain to it. The captain’s responsibility was to take ownership of the creation of the deliverable. We would usually have a discussion about the deliverable first and then the captain would use the results of that discussion and their own research/work to put the deliverable together. The other team member would give feedback when the captain had completed the first draft. Based on the feedback and the resulting discussions, the captain would make any refinements and additions to the deliverable before finally submitting it.
